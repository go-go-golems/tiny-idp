## Testing Your Implementations

The OpenID Foundation believes that proof-of-conformance provides an essential 
[safeguard](https://openid.net/uns-dpi-day-elizabeth-garber-on-standards-as-safeguards/) in 
identity systems and ecosystems. That’s why we enable developers to [test 
conformance](https://www.certification.openid.net/login.html) and certify their implementations.

### Conformance Testing

The free suite of [conformance tests](https://www.certification.openid.net/login.html) are 
available to anyone and enables developers to assess how well their products and implementations 
conform to our most mature, stable standards and profiles. This helps ensure interoperability and 
ensures, for example, that the security features of a given standard have been built correctly.

For more information on how to use these tools and run specific tests, visit our [Developer 
Resources](https://openid.net/certification/how-to-certify-your-implementation/) page.

### Certification

Implementers may want to certify conformance to a standard. This is particularly relevant if a 
company wishes to signal to its customers that a product is safe, secure, and capable of 
interoperating with others. Sometimes, certification is a pre-requisite to participating in an 
ecosystem (such as an open finance ecosystem).

**Self-Certification:** Using the OpenID Foundation’s conformance tests, implementers can follow 
a [self-certification process](https://openid.net/how-to-submit-your-certification-request/). Once 
complete, Certified implementations can use the “ [OpenID Certified](https://openid.net/mark/) 
” certification mark.

**Independent Certification:** In order to support ecosystems and implementers at-scale, the OpenID 
Foundation is developing a network of partners who will act as [Authorized 
Auditors](https://openid.net/oidf-sets-the-stage-for-independent-oversight-of-conformance-testing/) 
and [Testing Service 
Providers](https://openid.net/leading-organisations-join-oidf-independent-conformance-test-program/)
. This page will be updated as those services mature.
